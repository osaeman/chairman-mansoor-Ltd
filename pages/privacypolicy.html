PRIVACY POLICY

We at Chairman Mansoor Ltd UK are dedicated to protecting and maintaining our visitors' privacy.

This Privacy Policy describes what happens to any personal information you give us or that we learn about you from using our website.

We do update this Policy from time to time so please do review this Policy regularly.

Information We Collect

In order to operate and maintain our website, we might gather and use the following information about you:

  1. Data regarding how you use our website, including specifics about your visits, like the pages you view and the resources you use. Information of this type includes location, traffic, and other communication data.
  2. Information that you voluntarily supplied. For instance, when you register to receive information or make a purchase.
  3. Data that you submit to us through any method of communication.

Use of Cookies

Cookies give information about the computer that a visitor is using. When necessary, we may use cookies to collect data about your computer that will help us make improvements to our website.

We might use the cookie to collect data about your overall internet usage. These cookies are downloaded to your computer and saved there on the hard drive when they are used. This kind of data won't allow anyone to identify you specifically. These are statistical data. There are no identifiable personal details in this statistical data.

If you'd like, you can change your computer's settings to reject all cookies. To accomplish this, simply turn on your computer's reject cookies setting.

We have no control over cookies used by our advertisers. If such cookies are used, they are downloaded when you click on our website's advertisements.

Use of Your Information

We use the information that we collect from you to provide our services to you. In addition to this we may use the information for one or more of the following purposes:

  1. To provide information to you that you request from us relating to our products or services.
  2. To give you information about additional products that might be of interest to you. Such extra information will only be given in cases where you have given permission to receive it.
  3. To inform you of any changes to our website, services or goods and products.

If you have previously bought products or services from us, we might send you information about comparable products or services, as well as other products and services, that you might find interesting.

If you have given your consent in advance, we may permit specific third parties to use your information so they can contact you with offers of unrelated products and services that we think you might find interesting. If you have given such consent, you are free to withdraw it at any moment.

Storing Your Personal Data

We may need to send the data we collect from you to locations outside of the European Union in order to process and store it in order to run our website. By giving us your personal information, you consent to its processing, storing, and transfer. We make every effort to guarantee that all appropriate measures are implemented to guarantee the safe handling and storage of your data.

Unfortunately, there is never complete security when sending information over the internet; it is possible for it to be intercepted. Please send any data you choose to send us electronically at your own risk, as we are unable to guarantee its security.

Disclosing Your Information

We promise not to share your personal information with third parties unless necessary to comply with this Privacy Policy and under the situations listed below:

  1. In the event that we sell any or all of our business to the buyer.
  2. Where we are legally required by law to disclose your personal information.
  3. To further fraud protection and reduce the risk of fraud.

Third Party Links

This website occasionally contains links to external websites. The fact that we offer a link does not imply that we support or agree with the website's privacy policies. Prior to providing them with any personal information, you should review their privacy statement.

Access to Information

You have the right to access any information we hold about you in compliance with the Data Protection Act of 1998. Please be aware that in order to cover our costs in providing you with the information, we reserve the right to charge a fee of £10.

Contacting Us

Please do not hesitate to contact us regarding any matter relating to this Privacy Policy at Sterling House – contact details.

Cookies and how they Benefit You

Like nearly all websites, ours makes use of cookies to give you the best possible experience. When you browse websites, cookies—small text files—are downloaded to your computer or mobile device. Additional information about cookies is available at http://www.allaboutcookies.org/

Our cookies help us:

  • Make our website work as you’d expect
  • Improve the speed/security of the site
  • Permit sharing pages with Facebook and other social networks.

We do not use cookies to:

  • Obtain any personally identifying information (without your consent, of course)
  • Gather any private data (without your explicit consent)
  • Transfer information to advertising networks
  • Give third parties access to personally identifiable data
  • Pay sales commissions

You can learn more about all the cookies we use below

Granting us permission to use cookies

We interpret this, along with your continued use of our website, to mean that you are okay with this if the settings on the software you are using to view this website (your browser) are set to accept cookies. You can find information on how to disable or remove cookies from our website below, but doing so may prevent our website from functioning as you would expect.

Third party functions

Like the majority of websites, ours also has third-party functionality. A YouTube video that is embedded is a typical example.

Disabling these cookies will likely break the functions offered by these third parties

Third Party Sites

Please be aware that this website contains links to websites owned by third parties, like Facebook. Cookies might be set when you click on a link from our website to another website. Any such third-party website's use of cookies is outside our control. We strongly advise you to read the privacy and cookie policies on each of these websites as they will differ. We disclaim all liability for the actions or information found on websites owned by third parties.

Turning Cookies Off

Generally, you can disable cookies by changing your browser's settings to prevent it from accepting them (see how to do that here). However, since cookies are a common feature of the majority of contemporary websites, doing so will probably restrict the functionality of ours and a significant portion of other websites worldwide.

It's possible that your worries about cookies have to do with "spyware." Instead of turning off cookies in your browser, you might discover that anti-spyware software accomplishes the same goal by automatically erasing cookies that are deemed intrusive. Read more about using antispyware software to manage cookies..
